Canvas palette — 10 picks (ink / fill / wash)

The color vocabulary is 10 picks, one per hue. Each pick resolves to three stops:

  • Ink (dark) — the picker swatch, and every stroke: shape border, connector line, section chip border. What you pick is what lines get.
  • Fill (light) — shape fill, sticky fill, section chip fill.
  • Wash (lightest) — the section background tint, so sections always sit lighter than the objects on them.

Inks are line-safe: chosen so a 2px stroke reads on the #F5F5F5 board (mustard yellow #E8A302 rather than FigJam's bright #FFCD29, deep orange #EB7500, mid gray #757575). Legacy <hue>-soft ids migrate to the bare hue id.

Exceptions

  • White: picker swatch shows #FFFFFF (with a contrast ring), but all its strokes use the neutral gray ink #757575. Its section chip is #E6E6E6 / #C4C4C4 so it doesn't vanish on the white tint.
  • Gray section chip borders with the full ink #757575 (the derived light-gray chip border would be invisible on the near-white wash).
  • Teal: fill lightened from the legacy pastel #5AD8CC to #C6FAF6 so it sits in the same pastel band as the other fills; its wash is derived.
